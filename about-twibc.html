Our Story

History of TWI Bible College

Thy Word International Bible College Bataan (TWIBC) was established in Balanga, Bataan, with a clear vision to raise up Spirit-filled men and women who will faithfully proclaim the Gospel of Jesus Christ. Founded upon the principles of the Assemblies of God, TWIBC was birthed out of a desire to equip pastors, missionaries, evangelists, and lay leaders with sound biblical training rooted in Pentecostal doctrine and practice.

From its humble beginnings, the College has remained steadfast in its mission to serve the local church and the greater Body of Christ. Guided by the conviction that "the harvest is plentiful but the laborers are few" (Matthew 9:37), TWIBC has consistently sought to train leaders who will go into all the world, empowered by the Holy Spirit, to fulfill the Great Commission.

Throughout the years, TWIBC has offered programs ranging from foundational studies in theology to advanced ministerial training, always emphasizing the full Gospel message and the dynamic work of the Holy Spirit. Many of its graduates are now faithfully serving as pastors, missionaries, and church workers both in the Philippines and abroad.

Today, under the leadership of Rev. Orlando "Dolan" Acda, TWIBC continues to expand its influence as a training center of excellence in Bataan. With a strong commitment to the Assemblies of God Statement of Fundamental Truths, the College upholds the authority of God's Word, the centrality of Christ, the necessity of Spirit-baptism, and the call to holy living and active service in God's kingdom.

Our Mission & Vision

🎯
Our Mission
Equipping Spirit-Filled Leaders

Thy Word International Bible College (TWIBC) Bataan exists to equip Spirit-filled ministerial leaders for the Assemblies of God churches in the Philippines and beyond, and to serve as a training center that empowers the Body of Christ to fulfill the Great Commission.

Specialized training is offered in Bible, theology, pastoral ministry, Christian education, church planting, evangelism, and missions, with an emphasis on the baptism in the Holy Spirit and the development of Spirit-empowered servant leaders.

Spirit-Empowered · Christ-Centered

Institutional Goals

Goals & Objectives

The goals of TWI Bible College are to provide comprehensive, Spirit-empowered theological education rooted in Assemblies of God doctrine and the authority of God's Word.

1

Provide an institution of learning for the Assemblies of God, while admitting non-AG students who are in harmony with the purposes and values of the school.

2

Implement a Christ-centered, biblically oriented curriculum that recognizes the Bible as the infallible and authoritative rule of faith and conduct.

3

Conduct a program designed to enrich learning experiences and promote effectiveness in ministry, for students from all educational backgrounds.

4

Provide a program of studies within the Pentecostal tradition of the Assemblies of God, giving emphasis to the person and work of the Holy Spirit in life and ministry.

5

Establish a curriculum to help students acquire the knowledge, skills, and attitudes for Christian leadership while encouraging inquiry and developing research skills.

6

Maintain an environment and offer guidance conducive to the development of spiritual life and character of students as servants of God.

7

Offer specialized ministry programs of continuing education for Christian service for both full-time ministry workers and lay leaders.

8

Conduct cross-cultural, missiological training in evangelism, church planting, and community development consistent with obedience to the Great Commission (Matt. 18:19–20).

Statement of Faith

Doctrinal Statement

TWI Bible College subscribes to the Statement of Fundamental Truths of the Assemblies of God. The College upholds the full statement found in the TWIBC Bataan constitution. The following is a summary of core beliefs.

The Bible is the inspired Word of God — the infallible rule of faith and conduct for all people.

There is one God, eternally existent in three persons — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

In the deity of our Lord Jesus Christ — His virgin birth, sinless life, miracles, vicarious death, bodily resurrection, and future return.

All humans have sinned, and the only means of being cleansed is through repentance and faith in the cleansing power of the blood of Christ.

Regeneration is absolutely essential for personal salvation. By Christ's atoning death, healing for the body is available through faith and prayer.

The baptism in the Holy Spirit may be received by all believers for effective life and service, enabling the Christian to live a holy life.

In the rapture of the Church at Christ's return — this is the blessed and living hope of all believers who walk in faith.

The bodily resurrection of the saved and the lost leads to everlasting life and to everlasting punishment, respectively.

Christ will rule for a thousand years — the millennial reign that establishes the eternal Kingdom of God on earth.

Affiliations

Partners & Accreditation

Thy Word International Bible College Bataan is firmly rooted in the Pentecostal heritage of the Assemblies of God and exists to serve as a training arm for equipping Spirit-filled leaders for ministry. Through key partnerships, TWIBC ensures academic credibility, spiritual accountability, and ministerial effectiveness.

Accreditation
Philippines General Council of the Assemblies of God (PGCAG)

TWIBC operates under the covering and doctrinal framework of the PGCAG. The College upholds the Assemblies of God Statement of Fundamental Truths and is committed to providing theological and ministerial training in line with Pentecostal faith and practice.

Membership
Asia Pacific Theological Association (APTA)

TWIBC maintains membership in the Asia Pacific Theological Association. This partnership ensures academic credibility, spiritual accountability, and ministerial effectiveness by connecting TWIBC to a wider network of Assemblies of God churches and ministries in the Philippines and abroad.
